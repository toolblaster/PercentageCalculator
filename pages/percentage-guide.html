1. How to Calculate Percentage (Step-by-Step)

The universal method to calculate percentage is to convert the percentage to a decimal and multiply by the whole. Here is the process:

Method
  1. Identify: Find the total number (e.g., 50).
  2. Convert: Divide percentage by 100 (20% → 0.20).
  3. Multiply: Decimal × Total (0.20 × 50 = 10).

2. What Is a Percentage?

A percentage is a number or ratio expressed as a fraction of 100. It is denoted using the percent sign (%). "Per cent" literally means "per 100".

Visual Concept

If a grid has 100 boxes and 50 are shaded, that is 50%. It standardizes comparisons between different totals.

Forms

  • Percentage: 25%
  • Decimal: 0.25
  • Fraction: 1/4

3. Core Percentage Formulas

These are the three most common scenarios found in daily life and standard percentage formula questions.

Find X% of Y

(Percentage ÷ 100) × Total

Ex: 20% of 500?

0.20 × 500 = 100

X is what % of Y?

(Part ÷ Whole) × 100

Ex: 42 out of 50?

(42 ÷ 50) × 100 = 84%

Reverse Percentage

Part ÷ (Percentage ÷ 100)

Ex: 30 is 15% of what?

30 ÷ 0.15 = 200

4. Percentage Increase and Decrease

Use these formulas when analyzing growth or loss over time, such as price changes or weight loss.

Percent Increase Formula

((New - Old) ÷ Old) × 100

Ex: $100 → $120

(20 ÷ 100) × 100 = 20% Increase

Percent Decrease Formula

((Old - New) ÷ Old) × 100

Ex: 200lbs → 180lbs

(20 ÷ 200) × 100 = 10% Decrease

5. Percentage Change vs Percentage Difference

Using the wrong formula here is a common error. Percent change implies a timeline (old to new). Difference compares two separate items.

Percentage Difference Formula

|A - B| ÷ ((A + B) / 2) × 100

We divide by the average of the two numbers to keep the comparison neutral.

Metric Formula Base
Percent Change Original Value
Percent Difference Average of Both

6. Percentage Points vs Percent

Definitions

%: A ratio.

Points (pp): Arithmetic difference.

Example: Interest Rate

Rate rises from 4% to 5%.

  • +1 Percentage Point (5-4)
  • +25% Increase (1/4)

8. Mental Math & Exam Tricks

The 10% Trick

Move decimal one place left.
10% of 450 is 45.

The 1% Trick

Move decimal two places left.
1% of 450 is 4.5.

Double & Halve

4% of 50 is hard?
Swap: 50% of 4 is 2.

15% Tip Trick

Find 10%, add half of it.
$40 → $4 + $2 = $6.

9. Common Percentage Mistakes

Dividing by Wrong Base

For change, always divide by the original value, never the new one.

Adding Percentages

A 20% discount + 20% discount is not 40% off. It is 36% (0.8 × 0.8).

Reverse Math Error

To remove 20% tax, divide by 1.20. Do not subtract 20%.

12. Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do I calculate a percentage?

Convert the percentage to a decimal (divide by 100) and multiply by the total. Or use our calculate percentage tool.

2. How do I calculate percentage increase?

Subtract the original from the new, divide by the original, and multiply by 100.

3. What is percentage difference?

It compares two values using their average as the base, rather than a specific starting point.

4. Can percentage exceed 100%?

Yes. If a number doubles, it increases by 100%. If it triples, it increases by 200%.

5. How do I calculate percent change?

Percent change measures the difference between a starting value and an ending value over time.

6. What is a percentage point?

It is the arithmetic difference between two percentages (e.g. 4% to 5% is 1 point).
