Learn Percentages – Complete Guide with Formulas & Examples

Master percentage math with formulas for increase, decrease, and difference. This guide provides step-by-step examples and shortcuts for fast calculations.

1. How to Calculate Percentage (Step-by-Step)

The universal method to calculate percentage is to convert the percentage to a decimal and multiply by the whole.

Method:

  1. Identify the total number (e.g., 50).
  2. Convert the percentage by dividing by 100 (20% → 0.20).
  3. Multiply the decimal by the total (0.20 × 50 = 10).

2. What Is a Percentage?

A percentage is a number or ratio expressed as a fraction of 100. "Per cent" literally means "per 100."

If a grid has 100 boxes and 50 are shaded, that represents 50%. It allows standardized comparison between different totals.

Forms:

  • Percentage: 25%
  • Decimal: 0.25
  • Fraction: 1/4

3. Core Percentage Formulas

Find X% of Y

Formula: (Percentage ÷ 100) × Total

Example: 20% of 500
0.20 × 500 = 100

X is what % of Y?

Formula: (Part ÷ Whole) × 100

Example: 42 out of 50
(42 ÷ 50) × 100 = 84%

Reverse Percentage

Formula: Part ÷ (Percentage ÷ 100)

Example: 30 is 15% of what?
30 ÷ 0.15 = 200

4. Percentage Increase and Decrease

Percent Increase Formula:

((New − Old) ÷ Old) × 100

Example: $100 → $120
(20 ÷ 100) × 100 = 20% increase

Percent Decrease Formula:

((Old − New) ÷ Old) × 100

Example: 200 → 180
(20 ÷ 200) × 100 = 10% decrease

5. Percentage Change vs Percentage Difference

Percent Change: Used when comparing over time (original to new).

Percentage Difference: Used when comparing two independent values.

Formula:
|A − B| ÷ ((A + B) / 2) × 100

Divide by the average to keep comparison neutral.

6. Percentage Points vs Percent

Percentage (%) is a ratio. Percentage Points (pp) are the arithmetic difference.

Example:
Rate rises from 4% to 5%.
That is +1 percentage point,
but a 25% increase relative to 4%.

7. Real-World Applications

  • Finance: ROI, APY, interest rates
  • Shopping: Discounts and sales tax
  • Business: Profit margins and growth
  • Exams: Scores and grading
  • Statistics: Polling and error margins

8. Mental Math & Exam Tricks

The 10% Trick:
Move the decimal one place left.
10% of 450 = 45

The 1% Trick:
Move decimal two places left.
1% of 450 = 4.5

Double & Halve:
4% of 50 → swap → 50% of 4 = 2

15% Tip Trick:
Find 10%, add half.
$40 → $4 + $2 = $6

9. Common Percentage Mistakes

  • Dividing by the wrong base: Always divide by the original value for change.
  • Adding percentages incorrectly: 20% off + 20% off ≠ 40% off. It becomes 36% (0.8 × 0.8).
  • Reverse math error: To remove 20% tax, divide by 1.20 — don’t subtract 20%.

10. Percentage Word Problems

Salary Raise:
$50,000 with 5% raise
50,000 × 1.05 = $52,500

Discount:
$1,000 item, 20% off
1,000 × 0.80 = $800

Population Growth:
10,000 people growing 10% yearly for 2 years
10,000 × 1.1 × 1.1 = 12,100

Exam Score:
Need 80% of 60 questions
0.80 × 60 = 48

11. Percentage Formula Cheat Sheet

  • Find X% of Y → (X ÷ 100) × Y
  • Y is what % of Z → (Y ÷ Z) × 100
  • % Increase → ((New − Old) ÷ Old) × 100
  • % Decrease → ((Old − New) ÷ Old) × 100
  • % Difference → |A − B| ÷ Avg(A,B) × 100

12. Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate a percentage?
Convert the percentage to a decimal and multiply by the total.

How do I calculate percentage increase?
Subtract original from new, divide by original, multiply by 100.

What is percentage difference?
It compares two values using their average as the base.

Can percentage exceed 100%?
Yes. If a number doubles, that is a 100% increase.

What is a percentage point?
It is the arithmetic difference between two percentages.
